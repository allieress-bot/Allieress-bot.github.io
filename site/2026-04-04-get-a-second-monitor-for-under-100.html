Get a Second Monitor for Under $100

Published 2026-04-04

**Introduction**

Working solely on your laptop screen can feel cramped and inefficient. You're constantly opening and closing windows, which wastes time and tires your eyes. Adding a second monitor can drastically improve your workflow and comfort, even if you're on a very tight budget. This guide will show you how to find and set up a cheap, functional second screen for under $100.

**Your Goal: More Screen Space, Less Money**

The aim is to expand your visual workspace by adding an external monitor to your existing laptop setup, all without breaking the bank. We're focusing on practical, budget-friendly options for everyday office or remote work.

**Why a Second Monitor Helps**

Having two screens lets you see more information at once. You can dedicate one screen to your main work document or video call and the other to your email, chat app, or reference materials. This means less clicking, less eye strain, and a smoother, more organized workday.

**What You'll Need**

* **A Used Monitor:** This is the biggest cost saver. We'll target a monitor that's perfect for standard office tasks.

* **The Right Cable:** You'll need a cable to connect your laptop to the monitor. HDMI is the most common and easiest option.

* **A Power Outlet:** The monitor will need its own power source.

**Finding Your Cheap Monitor**

This is where you'll save the most money. Forget buying new; used is the way to go for this budget goal.

* **Online Marketplaces:** Regularly check Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or other local online classifieds. Search terms like "monitor," "computer screen," or "LCD display."

* **Used Electronics Stores:** Goodwill, Salvation Army, local computer repair shops, or small electronics consignment stores often have monitors for sale.

* **Garage Sales/Estate Sales:** Keep an eye out for these. Sometimes people sell perfectly good electronics very cheaply just to clear them out.

**What to Look For**

When you find a used monitor, here's what to check:

* **Size:** Aim for 21 to 24 inches. This size offers good screen real estate without being too expensive or bulky.

* **Resolution:** 1080p (also called Full HD or 1920x1080) is ideal. It's clear enough for text and common on many older, affordable monitors.

* **Ports:** Make sure it has an HDMI port. Most modern laptops have HDMI. If not, check if it has DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA (though these might require a more specific adapter).

* **Condition:** Ask the seller if you can power it on. Look for dead pixels (tiny black dots that never light up), bright lines, or major scratches on the screen. Minor cosmetic scuffs on the plastic frame are usually fine.

**Getting the Right Cable**

Once you have your monitor, you need to connect it to your laptop.

* **Check Your Laptop's Ports:** Look at the sides of your laptop for a video output port. The most common is a trapezoid-shaped HDMI port. Some newer thin laptops use a USB-C port for video.

* **Check Your Monitor's Ports:** The monitor will have a corresponding port.

* **Buy the Cable:** If both have HDMI, you just need an HDMI-to-HDMI cable. These are very cheap, often under $10 online or at electronics stores.

* **Adapters (If Needed):** If your laptop has USB-C and your monitor has HDMI, you'll need a "USB-C to HDMI adapter" or "dongle." These typically cost $15-$25. If your laptop has DisplayPort or Mini DisplayPort, you'll need an adapter for those too.

**Connecting and Setting Up**

This part is usually very simple.

1. **Plug in Power:** Connect the monitor's power cord to a wall outlet.

2. **Connect Video:** Plug one end of your video cable (e.g., HDMI) into your laptop and the other into your monitor.

3. **Turn On:** Power on both your laptop and the monitor.

4. **Adjust Display Settings:** Your laptop should automatically detect the new screen. If not, go to your computer's display settings (search "display settings" in Windows or System Settings > Displays on Mac). Choose "Extend these displays" or a similar option. You can also drag the screen icons in these settings to match how you've physically placed them on your desk.

**Tips for Success**

* **Be Patient:** Finding the perfect cheap monitor might take a few days of searching. Don't rush into a bad deal.

* **Negotiate:** Don't be afraid to politely offer a slightly lower price, especially if the monitor has a minor cosmetic flaw.

* **Clean It Up:** Used monitors might be dusty or have smudges. Give the screen and frame a good wipe-down with a microfiber cloth and screen cleaner (never use harsh chemicals directly on the screen).

* **Ergonomics:** Position the second monitor right next to your laptop screen, ideally at a similar height, to make it comfortable to look between them.

**What This Setup Solves**

You now have a much larger, more efficient workspace for less than the cost of a few takeout meals. This setup directly solves the problem of feeling cramped on a single laptop screen, allowing for better organization, easier multitasking, and less eye strain during your workday.

**What This Setup Doesn't Solve**

This budget setup is not for high-end graphic design, competitive gaming, or professional video editing. It's purely for improving everyday home office and remote work productivity on a minimal budget.
