Upgrade Your Laptop-Only Workspace

Starting remote work with just your laptop can be tough on your neck, back, and wrists. Hunching over a small screen and awkward keyboard causes strain fast. This guide focuses on fixing those basic ergonomic problems to make your laptop-only setup comfortable and productive for under $50.

Your main goal is to get your laptop screen to eye level and separate your keyboard and mouse. This simple change drastically improves posture and reduces strain.

**Step 1: Raise Your Laptop Screen (Cost: $0)**
The biggest problem with laptops is that the screen is too low, making you look down constantly.
*   **How:** Stack sturdy items under your laptop until the top of your screen is roughly level with your eyes when you sit upright. Good options include a stack of thick books, a shoebox, reams of paper, or even a sturdy baking pan.
*   **Why:** This prevents neck strain and encourages a straight spine. Make sure the laptop is stable and won't wobble or fall.

**Step 2: Get an External Keyboard (Cost: $10-$25)**
Now that your laptop screen is high up, its built-in keyboard is too high for comfortable typing. You need to bring your hands down.
*   **What to buy:** A basic wired USB keyboard. You don't need anything fancy. Most major office supply stores or online retailers have good options for cheap. Look for a full-size keyboard if you have space, or a compact one if your desk is small.
*   **Why:** This allows your hands and wrists to rest flat and comfortably on the desk, reducing strain that leads to carpal tunnel-like symptoms. Just plug it into an available USB port on your laptop.

**Step 3: Get an External Mouse (Cost: $5-$15)**
Using a laptop trackpad for hours can be slow and often forces your wrist into awkward positions.
*   **What to buy:** A basic wired USB mouse. Again, no need for gaming features or wireless bells and whistles. Pick one that feels comfortable in your hand.
*   **Why:** A mouse is much more precise and puts less stress on your wrist and fingers than a trackpad. Plug it into another available USB port.

**Total Estimated Cost: $15-$40**

That's it! With these three simple adjustments, you've transformed a cramped, painful laptop setup into a functional and much more ergonomic workspace. You'll notice a difference in comfort and focus immediately, making your work much more sustainable for long hours.
